OCD

What is OCD?

  • OCD, also known as Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder, is a mental health condition where a person has unwanted thoughts and feels the need to repeat certain actions or behaviors.

    • What causes it?

  • OCD can be caused by a mix of brain chemistry, genetics (family history), and high levels of anxiety or stress. It may also develop after difficult or stressful life experiences.

    • How to prevent it?

  • OCD cannot always be prevented, but you can reduce symptoms by managing stress, practicing healthy habits, and getting support from a therapist or trusted adult. Learning ways to control anxious thoughts can also help.
