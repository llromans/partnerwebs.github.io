Testimony

I want to share my story about mental health and what I’ve learned from my own experiences. Mental health affects everyone, even when we don’t always notice it. For me, it’s about how I think, feel, and handle everyday life. When my mental health is in a good place, I can manage stress, stay focused, and enjoy life more. But when I struggle, even simple things can feel overwhelming, and it’s easy to get stuck in negative thoughts. Through my experiences, I’ve realized that mental health challenges can take many forms. Some people face depression, feeling low or hopeless. Others struggle with anxiety or stress, feeling worried or tense all the time. There are also conditions like ADHD, OCD, PTSD, or schizophrenia that can affect how someone thinks, feels, or behaves, and even dementia, which can change memory and daily functioning. Learning about these conditions helped me understand that everyone’s experience is different, and that struggling doesn’t mean you’re alone or weak. What really helped me was talking to someone I trust, like a friend, family member, or teacher. Even small steps, like writing down my feelings or asking for guidance, made a big difference. I also found that keeping a healthy routine—sleeping well, staying active, eating properly, and doing activities I enjoy—supports my mental health and helps me feel more confident in myself. Sharing my story has taught me that taking care of your mental health is just as important as taking care of your body. Asking for help, learning about conditions, and practicing self-care can make a huge difference. Mental health matters for everyone, and no one has to go through it alone.
